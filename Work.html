Designing a deployment console for smart-contract-powered distributed applications

Problem/Opportunity:

Digital Asset offers Daml, a platform for developing multi-party distributed applications. While developing multiple proofs of concept for clients, and observing users getting started with the Daml platform, we found a unique opportunity to provide a fully managed cloud environment to enable our customers to get to market much quicker by skipping over having to construct and maintain their own infrastracture.

Solution:

A lean team was built internally to develop this idea. I helped drive a lot of the initial discussions to define the target persona, and value proposition for this new project. Over time, I lead design for the information architecture and user interface of the offering. I was able to work extremely closely with engineers and even help with implementation and visual styling in code. Information Architecture that supports the users mental model and the local developer experience

Throughout this project, a robust and thoroughly maintained information architecture map has been crucial for developing a tool that users can immediately recognize and be productive with. Conducting research and stakeholder interviews to understand the Daml stack, the local developer experience and common patterns that developers are used to informed the decisions that were made to establish where information is located and arranged within the digital space.

Prototyping - From paper to code

From low fidelity sketches to communicate high level ideas, to high fidelity mock-ups for validating our approach through rapid testing. When we had high confidence in a solution to a problem, developers and I simply collaborated in code.

Responsive and accessible design

The platform was buit to be fully responsive to support users in a variety of contexts. The design system, navigation patterns and interaction models established over time comply with WCAG 2.0 requirements to ensure the tool is accessible to all.

2015-2018

IBM Projects - Designing tools to aid healthcare providers make clinical decisions

Watson for Oncology

Watson for Oncology is a clinical decision support tool that aggregates and analyzes data from published medical literature, guidelines, and expert training by Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, to assist oncologists in making the best possible treatment decision for their patients.

In this role I developed a robust subject matter experttise in oncology and designing for cognitive systems. I worked collaboratively with a team that fluctuated between 2-6 designers at a time (Design research, Visual design and UX design), as well as Offering management, Engineering teams, a Localization Design team in China, and more.

As a UX designer, I've made sense of complex information architecture problems, iterated on interaction models, and assisted in conducting research locally and internationally to uncover user needs and market opportunities.

Watson for Clinical Trial Matching

Watson for Clinical Trial Matching is a tool that analyzes a patients medial record and diagnosis, and matches the patient with potential clinical trials. Along with researchers, we explored ways in which we could develop tools and workflows to aid clinical trial coordinators find the right candidates to fill trials. As part of this work, we were also tasked with prototyping how Watson for Clinical Trial Matching may integrate with Watson for Oncology to improve care coordination and ultimately, patient outcomes.

While in School

Lootbox - Re-purposing materials throughout campus

Lootbox is a student project that aimed to solve the problem of scrap materials crowding the halls of our art school's buildings. We created a system for re-purposing materials throughout the campus by setting designated boxes in the most scrap-heavy areas and creating an app to showcase and find sought-after materials.

Time: 5 weeks

Team: 4 designers

My role: Interaction Designer

Special Project

Hexos IP - A reliable and transparent B2C IP Renewal Platform

Hexos IP is a B2C platform for small enterprises to renew their intelectual property. Due to the complex current state of renewing IP in different countries, with different rates and renewal systems, it is not uncommon for current services to over-charge and provide no sense of certainty and control to their customers on the status and pricing of their IP portfolio.

Time: 1 year

Team: 1 Designer, 3 Engineers, Business & Founders

My role: UX Designer.
